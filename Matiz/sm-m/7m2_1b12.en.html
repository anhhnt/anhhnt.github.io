Oil Pump

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the timing belt and timing belt tensioner. Refer to "Timing Belt"in this section.
  3. Remove the camshaft gear.
    • Remove the bolt (1).
    • Remove the camshaft gear from the camshaft (2).



  4. Remove the timing belt rear cover.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Remove the timing belt rear cover (2).



  5. Remove the oil pan. Refer to "Oil Pan" in this section.
  6. Remove the oil pump strainer.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Remove the oil pump strainer with oil seal (2).



  7. Remove the crankshaft gear and oil pump bolts.
    • Remove the crankshaft gear from the crankshaft (1).
    • Remove the oil pump bolts (2).



  8. Remove the A/C compressor bracket.
    • Remove the A/C compressor bracket bolt (a).
  9. Remove the oil pump (1).
    • Clean the oil pump and engine block gasket mating surfaces.
    • Remove the oil seal from the oil pump (b).
    • Inspect the oil seal for damage.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install a new oil pump gasket. If the crankshaft front oil seal is damaged, replace it with new oil seal.
  3. When installing the crankshaft front oil seal to the oil pump, make the oil seal groove faced (a) toward the oil pump case contact surface (b) as shown in the picture.
    4. a. Oil seal groove.
    b. Oil pump case contact surface.



  4. Install the oil pump strainer and bolts.

    5. Tighten
    Tighten the oil pump strainer bolts to 9-12 N•m (80- 106 lb-in).




  5. Install the oil pump, oil pump cover bolts, and timing belt rear cover bolts.
    • Oil pump cover bolts (a).
    • Timing belt rear cover bolts (b).

    Tighten
    Tighten the oil pump cover bolts and the timing belt rear cover bolts to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).




  6. Install the camshaft gear and bolt.

    7. Tighten
    Tighten the camshaft gear bolt to 50-60 N•m (36-44 lb-ft).

Cylinder Head and Gasket

Removal Procedure

  1. Relieve the fuel system pressure. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  2. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  3. Remove the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
  4. Remove the canister solenoid valve. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  5. Drain the engine coolant. Refer to Section 1D, Engine Cooling.
  6. Remove the PCV valve. Refer to "PCV Hose and Valve" in this section.
  7. Remove the DIS(Direct Ignition System). Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.



  8. Disconnect the cable, electrical connector and vacuum tube.
    • Loose the lock nut and disconnect the throttle cable (1).
    • Disconnect the Manifold Absolute Pressure(MAP) sensor connector (2).
    • Disconnect the brake vacuum tube(3).
    • Disconnect the Intake Manifold Temperature(IAT) sensor connector (4).
    • Disconnect the DIS connector (5).
    • Remove the ignition cables (6).
    • Disconnect the IACV connector (7).
    • Disconnect the TPS connector (8).
    • Disconnect the canister solenoid tube (9).



  9. Disconnect the Injector connector. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  10. Disconnect the cam sensor connector (1).
  11. Disconnect the coolant temperature sensor connector (2).
  12. Disconnect the EGR connector (3).



  13. Remove the intake manifold subsidiary bracket.
    • Remove the upper bolt (1).
    • Remove the lower nut (2).



  14. Disconnect the coolant hose (1).



  15. Remove the timing belt. Refer to "Timing Belt" in this section.
  16. Remove the camshaft gear.
    • Remove the bolt (1).
    • Remove the camshaft gear from the camshaft (2).



  17. Remove the timing belt rear cover.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Remove the rear cover from the cylinder head andblock (2).
  18. Remove the catalytic converter from the exhaust manifold.
    • Remove the nuts from the catalytic converter.



  19. Remove the cylinder head cover.
    • Remove the cylinder head cover hexagon bolts (1).
    • Remove the cylinder head cover with gasket (2).



  20. Remove the cylinder head assembly.
    • Gradually loosen all of the cylinder head bolts in the sequence shown.
    • Remove the cylinder head bolts (1).
    • Remove the cylinder head assembly from the engine block, with the intake/exhaust manifold, throttle body, distributor housing and cylinder head gasket (2).
    • Inspect the cylinder head gasket for damaged.
    • Clean the gasket surfaces of the cylinder head and the engine block.
    • Clean the cylinder head bolts.






Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install new cylinder head gasket.
  3. Install the cylinder head bolts in the sequence shown.

    4. Tighten
    Tighten the cylinder head bolts to 65-70 N•m (48-52 lb-ft).




  4. Install the cylinder head cover hexagon bolts.

    5. Tighten
    Tighten the cylinder head cover hexagon bolts to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).




  5. Install the bolts and nuts.

    6. Tighten

    • Tighten the front exhaust pipe nuts (a) to 35-45 N•m (25-33 lb-ft).
    • Tighten the timing belt rear cover bolts (b) to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).



    • Tighten the camshaft gear bolt to 50-60 N•m (36-44 lb-ft).



Engine Mount Damping Block

Tools Required

DW 110-021 Engine Assembly Support Fixture.

Removal Procedure

  1. Install the engine assembly support fixture DW 110- 021.
    • Remove the cowl panel weatherstrip.
    • Remove the exhaust manifold heat shield.
    • Position the engine assembly support fixture DW 110-021 on the cowl panel and the front upper panel on the center.
  2. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  3. Remove the right-hand head lamp assembly. Refer to Section 9B, Lighting System.



  4. Remove the washer fluid tank neck (1).
  5. Remove the surge tank bolts (2).

    6. Notice : To remove the ABS module bracket nut, you can pull aside the surge tank.

  6. Remove the ABS Moudule bracket bolts and Nut (3)
  7. Detach the brake pipes from the clip (4).

    8. Notice : After remove the surge tank bolts and ABS Module bolts/nut, you can pull surge tank and ABS Module aside to make a space to remove the engine mount damping block.

    Caution : If too heavy pulling the surge tank and ABS Module aside, it may be damaged to them.




  8. Remove the power steering hose cliping nut (1).
  9. Remove the coolant hose cliping bolt (2).

    10. Notice : After remove the coolant hose cliping bolt and power steering hose cliping nut, you can pull the coolant hose and power steering hose aside to make a space to remove the engine mount damping block.

    Caution : If too heavy pulling the coolant hose and power steering hose aside, it may be damaged to them.




  10. Remove the intermediate bracket nuts.
    • Remove the intermediate nut from the lower side of vehicle (1).
    • Remove the intermediate nut from the upper vehicle (2).



  11. Remove the engine mount damping block with the intermediate bracket attached.
    • Remove the engine mount damping block bolts.

    Important : Loosen the bolts adjusting the engine assembly support fixture up and down slightly.

    Notice : If the bolts are loosened without adjusting the engine assembly support fixture, they could be damaged.




Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install the engine mount damping block with the intermediate bracket attached.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the engine mount damping block bolts to 45-55 N•m (33-41 lb-ft).




  3. Install the intermediate bracket nuts.
    • Install the intermediate bracket nut to the lower side of vehicle (a).
    • Install the intermediate bracket nut to upper side of vehicle (b).

    Tighten
    Tighten the intermediate nuts to the upper/lower side of vehicle to 58-68 N•m (43-50 lb-ft).




Transmission Mount Damping Block

Tools Required

DW 110-021 Engine Assembly Support Fixture.

Removal Procedure

  1. Install the engine assembly support fixture DW 110- 021.
    • Remove the cowl panel weatherstrip.
    • Remove the exhaust manifold heat shield.
    • Position the engine assembly support fixture DW 110-021 on the cowl panel and the front upper panel on the center.



  2. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  3. Remove the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
  4. Remove the battery and tray. Refer to Section 1E, Engine Electronical.
  5. Remove the transmission mount damping block.
    • Remove the bolts and nuts.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install the transmission mount damping block.
    • Install the bolts and nuts.

    Tighten
    Tighten the transmission mount damping block bolts and nuts to 45-55 N•m (33-41 lb-ft).




Reaction Rod

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the reaction rod.
    • Remove the bolt and nut (1).
    • Remove the reaction rod bolts (2).



Installation Procedure

  1. Install the reaction rod.
    • Install the reaction rod bolts and nuts (a).
    • Install the reaction rod to body bracket bolts (b).

    Tighten

    • Tighten the reaction rod bolts and nuts (a) to 75-85 N•m (55-63 lb-ft).
    • Tighten the reaction rod to body bracket bolt (b) to 50-60 N•m (36-44 lb-ft).

Engine Assembly

Tools Required

EN-47935 Engine & Transaxle Assembly Support Remover/Installer.

Removal Procedure

    • Discharge the air conditioning(A/C) system, if equipped.
    • Drain the power steering oil, if equipped.
  1. Relieve the fuel system pressure. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  2. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  3. Remove the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
  4. Remove the battery and tray. Refer to Section 1E, Engine Electronic.
  5. Remove the canister solenoid valve. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  6. Drain the engine coolant. Refer to Section 1D, Engine Cooling.
  7. Remove the PCV valve. Refer to "PCV Hose and Valve" in this section.
  8. Remove the DIS(Direct Ignition System). Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.



  9. Disconnect the cable, electrical connector and vacuum tube.
    • Loose the lock nut and disconnect the throttle cable (1).
    • Disconnect the Manifold Absolute Pressure(MAP) sensor connector (2).
    • Disconnect the brake vacuum tube (3).
    • Disconnect the Intake Manifold Temperature(IAT) sensor connector (4).
    • Disconnect the DIS connector (5).
    • Remove the ignition cables (6).
    • - Disconnect the IACV connector (7).
    • Disconnect the TPS connector (8).
    • Disconnect the canister solenoid tube (9).



  10. Disconnect the Injector connector. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  11. Disconnect the cam sensor connector (1).
  12. Disconnect the coolant temperature sensor connector (2).



  13. Disconnect the power steering pipe and hose (1) and the A/C pipes (2).



  14. Disconnect the surge tank hose.



  15. Disconnect the coolant hoses.
  16. Remove the cooling fan. Refer to Section 1D, Engine Cooling.



  17. Disconnect the select and shift cable.
    • Remove the cable pin (1).
    • Remove the washer (2).
    • Disconnect the select and shift cable (3).
    • Remove the E-ring (4).



  18. Drain the transmission oil.
    • Remove the oil drain plug to drain oil.
  19. Disconnect the clutch cable.
    • Loosen the cable adjusting nut (1).
    • Disconnect the cable from the transmission mount hole (2).
    • Remove the clutch cable mount (3).



  20. Disconnect the speedometer cable.
    • Loosen the nut (1).
    • Disconnect the cable (2).
    • Disconnect the vehicle speed sensor electrical connector, if equipped.
  21. Remove the front exhaust pipe. Refer to Section 1G,Engine Exhaust.



  22. Disconnect the electrical connector and clip around the starter.
    • Disconnect the alternator IC connector (1).
    • Remove the alternator B+ cable nut (2).
    • Remove the starter solenoid nut (3).
    • Remove the ground cable bolt (4).
    • Remove the oil pressure switch cable (5).
    • Remove the harness clip and knock sensor connector clip from the cable bracket (6).
    • Remove the speed-moter driven gear connector (7).



  23. Remove the left/right drive axle. Refer to Section 3B, Manual Transaxle Drive Axle.
  24. Remove the reaction rod. Refer to the "Reaction Rod" in this section.
  25. Remove the radiator crossmember assembly.
    • Remove the front bump lower screws (1).
    • Remove the radiator crossmember bolts (2).



  26. Disconnect the heater core inlet/outlet hoses.



  27. Support the engine & transaxle assembly with Engine & Support Fixture EN-47935.



  28. Remove the engine mount nuts.
    • Remove the intermediate nut from the lower side vehicle (1).
    • Remove the intermediate nut from the upper side vehicle (2).



  29. Remove the transmission mount nuts.



  30. Remove the engine/transmission assembly.
    • Lower the special tool slowly (1).
    • Remove the engine/transmission assembly (2).



  31. Remove the transmission from the engine/transmission assembly. Refer to Section 5B1, Five-Speed Manual Transaxle.
  32. Remove some parts equipped with the engine assembly. Refer to "Unit Repair" in this section.

Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
    • Install som parts removed from the engine assembly. Refer to "Unit Repair" in this section.
    • Connect the transaxle assembly with the engine assembly. Refer to Section 5B1, Five-Speed Manual Transaxle.



  2. Install the transmission mount nuts.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the transmission mount nuts to 50-60 N•m (36-44 lb-ft).




  3. Install the engine mount nuts.
    • Install the intermediate nut to the lower side of vehicle (1).
    • Install the intermediate nut to the upper side of vehicle (2).

    Tighten
    Tighten the intermediate nuts to the upper/lower side of vehicle to 58-68 N•m (43-50 lb-ft).




  4. Connect the heater core inlet/outlet hoses.



  5. Install the radiator crossmember assembly.
    • Install the bolt and screw (b).

    Tighten
    Tighten the radiator crossmember bolts (a) to 30-50 N•m (22-36 lb-ft).

  6. Install the reaction rod. Refer to "Reaction Rod" in this section.
  7. Install the left/right drive axle. Refer to Section 3B, Manual Transaxle Drive Axle.



  8. Install the starter solenoid nut, the ground bolt and the alternator B(+) cable nut.

    9. Tighten

    • Tighten the starter solenoid nut (a) to 7.5-9.5 N•m (66-84 lb-in).
    • Tighten the ground bolt (b) to 12-15 N•m (106-133 lb-in).
    • Tighten the alternator B(+) cable nut (c) to 8-10 N•m (71-89 lb-in).



  9. Install the front exhaust pipe. Refer to Section 1G, Engine Exhaust.
  10. Connect the select and shift cable.
    • Install the cable pin (1).
    • Install the washer (2).
    • Connect the select and shift cable (3).
    • Install the E-ring (4).



  11. Install the cooling fan. Refer to Section 1D, Engine Cooling.
  12. Connect the surge tank hose.



  13. Connect the coolant hoses.



  14. Install the A/C pipes (b) and power steering pipe and hose (a).

    15. Tighten

    • Tighten the A/C pipes bolts (b) to 3-5 N•m (27-44 lb-in).
    • Tighten the power steering pipe bolt (a) to 21-35 N•m (15-26 lb-ft).



  15. Connect the cable, electrical connector and vacuum tube.
    • Tighten the lock nut and connect the throttle cable (1).
    • Connect the Manifold Absolute Pressure(MAP) sensor connector (2).
    • Connect the brake vacuum tube (3).
    • Connect the Intake Manifold Temperature(IAT) sensor connector (4).
    • Connect the DIS connector (5).
    • Install the ignition cables (6).
    • Connect the IACV connector (7).
    • Connect the TPS connector (8).
    • Connect the canister solenoid tube (9).
  16. Install the DIS(Direct Ignition System). Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  17. Install the canister solenoid valve. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  18. Install the battery and tray. Refer to Section 1E, Engine Electronic.
  19. Install the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
