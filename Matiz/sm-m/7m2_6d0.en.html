Steering Wheel Restoration

  1. Turn the steering wheel fast to the right and left and turn it slowly as the same way.
  2. Check if the steering wheel restoration for the wheel turning to right and the one to the left are same.
  3. Turn the steering wheel to 90_ about 1-2 seconds and loosen the steering wheel during the low speed driving.
  4. If the steering wheel restores above 70%, the steering wheel restoration is OK.
  5. If the restoration is poor, check the tire pressure and steering gear operation. And repair the fault.

Check the Turning Resistance of the Outer or Inner Tie Rod

  1. Turn the ball joint of the outer tie rod or the inner tie rod about 10 times.
  2. Use a spring balance to check the turning resistance of each ball joint.

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the wheel. Refer to Section 2E, Tires and Wheels.
  2. Disconnect the outer tie rod and from the steering knuckle.
    • Remove the cotter pin (1).
    • Remove the castellated nut (2).
    • Use a ball joint remover KM-507-B, separate the tie rod end (3).



  3. Remove the outer tie rod.
    • Mark the tie rod, tie rod lock nut and tie rod end (1).
    • Loosen the tie rod lock nut anticlockwise (2).
    • Loosen the outer tie rod (3).

Rack and Pinion Assembly

(Left-Hand Drive Shown, Right-Hand Drive Similar)

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the intermediate shaft lower pinch bolt. Refer to Section 6E, Steering Wheel And Column.
  2. Remove the wheels. Refer to Section 2E, Tires and Wheels.
  3. Disconnect the tie rod end from the steering knuckle.
    • Remove the cotter pin (1).
    • Remove the cotter tie rod nut (2).
    • Use a ball joint remover KM-507-B, seperate the outer tie rod (3).
  4. On vehicle equipped with auto transmission, remove the transmission under cover. Refer to Section 9N, Frame and Underbody.
  5. Remove the front exhaust muffler. Refer to Section 1G, Engine Exhaust.



  6. Remove the transmission bracket.
    • Remove the bolt and nut from the transmission side (1).
    • Loosen the bolt from the body side (2).



  7. Remove the rack and pinion assembly.
    • Remove the lower bracket bolts from left and right side of steering gear (1).
    • Remove the upper bracket bolts from left and right side of steering gear (2).

    Important : After moving the gear toward the passenger side and pulling the gear of the driver side into the underbody hole, remove the steering gear.




Installation Procedure

  1. Install the rack and pinion assembly.
    • Install the manual steering gear with upper bolts (a).

    Tighten
    Tighten the upper bolts of gear to 57 N•m (42 lb-ft).

    • Install the manual steering gear with lower bolts (b).

    Tighten
    Tighten the lower bolts of gear to 57 N•m (42 lb-ft).

    1. Install the transmission braket with bolts and nuts.



    Tighten
    Tighten the bolts and nuts to 80 N•m (59 lb-ft).

    1. Install the front exhaust muffler. Refer to Section 1G, Engine Exhaust.
    2. On vehicle equipped with auto transmission, install the transmission under cover. Refer to Section 9N, Frame and Underbody.
    3. Connect the outer tie rod and cotter pin to the steering knuckle.

    Tighten the outer tie rod nut to 45 N•m (33 lb-ft).

GENERAL DESCRIPTION AND SYSTEM OPERATION

Manual Rack and Pinion

The manual rack and pinion steering system consists of two main components: the rack and the pinion. The motion of the pinion is transferred through the pinion teeth that mesh with the teeth on the rack, which moves the rack. The force is then transmitted through the arms on the struts, which turn the wheels.
