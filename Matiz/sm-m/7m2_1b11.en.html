ON-VEHICLE SERVICE




Air Cleaner Assembly

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the air intake tube from the throttle body.
    • Disconnect the intake tube clamp (1).
    • Disconnect the hose from the intake tube (2).
  2. Remove the air cleaner assembly.
    • Remove the snorkel bolt (3).
    • Remove the air cleaner housing bolts(4).



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install the bolts (1).

    3. Tighten

    • Tighten the snorkel bolt to 7-9 N•m (62-80 lb-in).
    • Tighten the air cleaner housing bolts to 7-9 N•m (62-80 lb-in).
  3. Connect the intake tube clamp (2).
  4. Connect the intak tube (3).



Air Filter Element

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the air filter upper housing.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Disconnect the intake tube clamp (2).



  2. Remove the air filter element.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install the bolts (1).

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the bolts to 7-9 N•m (62-80 lb-in).

  3. Connect the intake tube clamp (2).



Resonator

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the right-hand head lamp assembly. Refer to Section 9B, Lighting System.
  3. Remove the resonator.
    • Remove the bolt and nut.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
  2. Install the resonator.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the bolt and nut to 5-7 N•m (44-62 lb-in).




Positive Crankcase Ventilation (PCV) Hose and Valve

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose.
    • Disconnect the hose from the intake tube (1).
    • Disconnect the hose from the cylinder head cover (2).
    • Disconnect the hose from the intake manifold lower (3).
    • Remove the PCV hose with valve (4).



  2. Remove the PCV valve.
    • Remove the PCV valve (1).
    • Inspect the tube for crack, inflation or damage.
    • Inspect the PCV valve orifice for damage. (When blowing up the valve using a mouth, air should be ventilated only in the direction of the arrow.)



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.

Intake Manifold

Removal Procedure

  1. Relieve the fuel system pressure. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  2. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  3. Remove the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
  4. Disconnect the radiator lower hose and drain coolant. Refer to Section 1D, Engine Cooling.
  5. Remove the throttle body assembly. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.

  6. Disconnect the PCV hose and valve. Refer to "PCV Hose and Valve" in this section.
  7. Remove the canister solenoid valve. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  8. Remove the fuel rail and injector. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.
  9. Remove the EGR system. Refer to Section 1F, Engine Controls.



  10. Disconnect the cable, electrical connector and vacuum tube.
    • Loose the lock nut and disconnect the throttle cable (1).
    • Disconnect the Manifold Absolute Pressure(MAP) sensor connector (2).
    • Disconnect the brake vacuum tube (3).
    • Disconnect the Intake Manifold Temperature(IAT) sensor connector (4).
    • Disconnect the DIS connector (5).
    • Remove the ignition cables (6).
    • Disconnect the IACV connector (7).
    • Disconnect the TPS connector (8).
    • Disconnect the canister solenoid tube (9).



  11. Remove the intake manifold subsidiary bracket.
    • Remove the upper bolt (1).
    • Remove the lower nut (2).



  12. Remove the intake manifold.
    • Remove the nuts (1).
    • Remove the intake manifold from the cylinder head (2).



  13. Disconnect the intake manifold gasket.
    • Disconnect the intake manifold gasket from the intake manifold (1).

    Important : Carefully remove the gasket pulling the gasket upper position (a).

    • Inspect the gasket for crack, hardening, inflation or damage.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.
    • When installing the gasket on the intake manifold, install the gasket correctly.
  2. Install the intake manifold with nuts in numerical order.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the nuts to 15-19 N•m (11-14 lb-ft).

    Important : First of all, tighten the bracket nut and then tighten the remaining nuts.




  3. Install the bolt/nut.

    4. Tighten

    • Tighten the intake manifold subsidiary bracket nut (a) to 18-22 N•m (13-16 lb-ft).
    • Tighten the intake manifold subsidiary bracket bolt (b) to 18-22 N•m (13-16 lb-ft).



Exhaust Manifold

Removal Procedure

    Caution : Caution : Do not remove when the exhaust manifold parts are hot.

  1. Remove the air cleaner assembly. Refer to "Air Cleaner Assembly" in this section.
  2. Disconnect the oxygen sensor connector (1).
  3. Remove the ignition cables (2).
  4. Remove the exhaust manifold heat shield.
    • Remove the bolts (3).



  5. Remove the catalytic converter from the exhaust manifold.
    • Remove the nuts.



  6. Remove the exhaust manifold.
    • Remove the bolts and nuts.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install the exhaust manifold.

    2. Tighten
    Tighten the exhaust manifold bolts and nuts 17-27 N•m (13-20 lb-ft).




  2. Install the catalytic converter.

    3. Tighten
    Tighten the catalytic converter nuts to 35-45 N•m (25-33 lb-ft).




  3. Install the exhaust manifold heat shield.

    4. Tighten
    Tighten the exhaust manifold heat shield bolts to 8-12 N•m (71-106 lb-in).




Timing Belt

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable.
  2. Remove the right headlamp. Refer to Section 9B, Lighting Systems.
  3. Remove the generator driver belt, A/C compressor driver belt and power steering driver belt, if equipped.
  4. Remove the timing belt front upper cover.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Remove the cover (2).



  5. Remove the right wheel. Refer to Section 2E, Tires and Wheels.
  6. Align the mark on the crankshaft gear and camshaft gear.
    • Using the crankshaft pulley bolt, rotate the crankshaft clockwise until the mark on the camshaft gear is aligned with the notch at the bottom of the rear timing belt cover (1).
    • Mark on the camshaft gear (a).
    • Notch at the bottom of the rear timing belt cover (b).



  7. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
    • Remove the crankshaft pulley bolt (1).
    • To remove the bolt, use a screwdriver such as the picture shown (a).
    • Remove the crankshaft pulley (2).



  8. Remove the oil level gauge guide tube and timing belt front lower cover.
    • Remove the bolt (1).
    • Remove the oil level gauge guide tube (2).
    • Remove the bolts (3).
    • Remove the timing belt front lower cover (4).



  9. Remove the timing belt.
    • Loosen the timing belt tensioner bolt (1).
    • Remove the timing belt (2).



Installation Procedure

  1. Install the timing belt tensioner bolt.

    2. Tighten
    Tighten the timing belt tensioner bolt to 15-23 N•m (11-17 lb-ft).




  2. Install the bolts.

    3. Tighten

    • Tighten the timing belt front lower cover bolts (a) to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).
    • Tighten the oil level gauge guide tube bolt (b) to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).
    • Tighten the timing belt front upper cover bolts (c) to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in).



  3. Install the crankshaft pulley bolt.
    • When installing the bolt, use the driver as shown in the picture (1).

    Tighten
    Tighten the crankshaft pulley bolt (a) to 65-75 N•m (48- 55 lb-ft).




Oil Pan

Removal Procedure

  1. Drain the engine oil from the engine crankcase.
  2. Remove the clutch housing lower plate.
    • Remove the bolts (1).
    • Remove the clutch housing lower plate (2).



  3. Remove the oil pan.
    • Remove the bolts and nuts (1).
    • Remove the oil pan from the engine block (2).
    • Clean the oil pan and engine block sealing surfaces.
    • Clean the oil pan bolts.
    • Clean the oil pan bolt holes in the engine block.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install in the reverse order of removal.

    2. Important : Install the oil pan within 5 minutes after applying liquid gasket to the oil pan.

  2. Install the bolts and nuts.

    3. Tighten

    • Tighten the oil pan bolts and nuts (a) to 9-12 N•m (80-106 lb-in) .
    • Tighten the clutch housing lower plate bolts (b) to 4-7 N•m (35-62 lb-in).
