2. TURN SIGNAL LIGHTS

  • Move turn signal switch up or down to the stop position to engage turn signals, and check for turn signal lights on.
  • When the turn is completed, the signal will cancel and the lever will return to the normal position.



3. HAZARD WARNING LIGHTS

  • Push the hazard warning flashes switch, and check that all turn signal lamps flash simultaneously.
  • Shorter flashes indicate the failure of lamp bulbs.
  • Push this switch again to turn them off.



4. BACK-UP LIGHTS

  • Select the gear shift lever to "R" position, and check for the lightening of back-up lights.

5. STOP LIGHTS

  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of upper side of stop lights located in the rear combination lamp assembly.
  • Foot the brake pedal, and check for the lightening of center high mounted stop lamp (option) located on the tailgate.

7. INSTRUMENT PANEL WARNING LIGHTS

Check for the operation of warning lights in instrument panel. (Ignition switch is in the "ON" position)



  1. ABS warning light*
  2. Malfunction indicator light
  3. High beam indicator
  4. Door open warning light
  5. Airbag warning light*
  6. Seat belt reminder
  7. Charging system warning light*
  8. Brake system warning light
  9. Engine oil pressure warning light
  10. Low fuel level warning light
  11. Turn signal/hazard warning flasher
  12. Front fog lamp indicator*
  13. Coolant temperature warning light
  14. Rear fog lamp indicator*
  • Brake system warning light
    • Comes on when the parking brake is applied. If this light comes on even when the parking brake is completely released, it may indicate that the brake fluid in the reservoir is low.
  • Engine oil pressure warning light
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and goes out after the engine is started. If the light comes on while driving, it indicates that the oil pressure is dangerously low.
  • Charging system warning light
    • Comes on when the ignition is switched on and should go out when the engine is started.
  • Malfunction indicator light
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and should go out shortly after the engine is started. If the indicator illuminates during normal running, a fault has occurred.
  • Turn signal/hazard warning flasher
    • Flashes to indicate operation of the external turn signal or hazard warning flasher.
  • High beam indicator
    • Illuminates when the headlight high beam is switched on.
  • Seat belt reminder
    • Comes on when the driver does not fasten the seat belt.
  • Low fuel level warning light
    • Comes on when the quantity of fuel in fuel tank is below approx. 5.5L.
  • ABS warning light (option)
    • Illuminates when the ignition is switched on and should go out after about 4 seconds. If this light illuminates while driving, a malfunction of ABS is detected.
  • Airbag warning light (option)
    • Flashes several times when the ignition is switched on and goes out. If this light illuminates or flashes while driving, a malfunction of airbag system is indicated.
  • Rear fog lamp indicator
    • Comes on when the rear fog lamp is turned on.
  • Door open warning light
    • Comes on when a door is either opened or not closed completely.
  • Coolant temperature waring light
    • Comes on when the engine coolant has over-heated.
  • Front fog lamp indicator (option)
    • Comes on when the front fog lamps are turned on.

8. DOME LIGHTS

15. WIPER AND WASHER OPERATION

  • Windshield wiper switch
    • Check that the wipers operate smoothly after moving the lever upwards with the ignition on.
    • OFF = off
    • INT = intermittent wiper operation(once every 4 seconds)
    • LOW = continuous wipe, slow speed
    • HIGH = continuous wipe, fast speed
    • – Misting function
    • To operate the wipers once, lightly move the lever towards the INT position and release it.



  • Windshield washer switch
    • Pull the lever toward the steering wheel for less than about 0.5 seconds and check if the washer fluid is sprayed onto the windshield with the windshield wipers not being operated. When you pull the lever for more than about 0.5 seconds, the washer fluid is sprayed onto the windshield while you pull the lever and then the windshield wipers are operated for several cycles.



16. TAILGATE WINDOW WIPER AND WASHER SWITCH (OPTION)

  • Second position (rocker position) = wiper and washer

19. CLOCK

Check the operation of the clock by pressing the following button with ignition switch in the "ON" position.
  • H : Hour adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the hour digits
  • M : Minute adjusting button
    • Press this button to adjust the minute digits
  • S : To reset the time by the time signal, press this button. For example, if this button is pressed while the time is between 8:00 and 8:29 the display is set to 8:00. If this button is pressed while the time is between 8:30 and 8:59, the display is set to 9:00.
    • 8:00 – 8:29 → 8:00
    • 8:30 – 8:59 → 9:00



20. DOOR LOCK

Check doors being locked or unlocked from outside by using the key.
All doors can be locked or unlocked from the inside by pushing or pulling the door lock buttons located on the window sill on each door. The driver's door cannot be locked from the outside by pushing the door lock button, thus preventing the driver from being inadvertently locked out.






  • Central door locking system (option)
    • All doors can be simultaneously locked or unlocked when the driver's door is locked or unlocked.
  • Child security door locks

28. IMMOBILIZER SYSTEM (OPTION)

Immobilizer system prevents the vehicle from being started by unauthorized persons by isolating the power supply to the ignition system, fuel pump and fuel injectors and it is a mechanical ignition key with integrated transponder, which is electronically coded.
Immobilizer indicator can be operated in the following conditions :
  • Stays on when the ignition key without transponder is placed in the ON position.
  • Blinks when the ignition key with transponder that key coding is invaild is placed in the ON position.

29. KEYLESS ENTRY SYSTEM WITH ANTITHEFT FUNCTION(OPTION) <GENERAL AREA MODELS ONLY>

Check the operation of the keyless entry system by using the transmitter.

Door lock and antitheft mode activation

Press the lock button on the transmitter and release it.
LED on the transmitter flashes once.
Then all the doors are locked, hazard warning lamps flashes once with chirp sound and the antitheft mode is activated.
If the ignition switch is placed in the "ACC" position, the antitheft mode and hazard warning lamps do not operate.

Door unlock and antitheft mode deactivation

Press the unlock button on the transmitter and release it.
LED on the transmitter flashes once.
Then all the doors are unlocked, hazard warning lamps flash twice and the antitheft mode is deactivated.

Auto door lock

If the door is not opened or the engine is not started within 30 seconds after unlocking the door with the transmitter when the vehicle is under the antitheft mode, all the doors are automatically locked and the antitheft mode is switched on.

Antitheft alarm function

Under antitheft mode, the system provides visual and audible alarm when any of the doors, tailgate or the hood are opened without using the key or pressing the unlock button on the transmitter.
To stop the alarm sound, press any button on the transmitter.
