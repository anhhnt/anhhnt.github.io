  • Replace the power steering pump.

  • Replace the power steering pump.

Growling Noise in Steering Pump
  • Restricted flow of the pump hoses and the steering gear pipes.
  • Clean out the pipes and the hoses.
  • Replace the pipes and the hoses as needed.
  • Scores, cracks, or breaks of the pressure plate, the pump ring, the thrust plate, and the rotor.
  • Replace the power steering pump.
  • Improperly contacted power steering hose with the body.
  • Secure the pump hose in a clamp away from the body.
  • Insufficient power steering fluid level.
  • Fill the power steering pump reservoir.
Ratting Noise in the Steering Pump
  • Contaminated air in the power steering fluid.
  • Bleed the power steering system.
  • Improperly contacted power steering hose with the body.
  • Secure the pump hose in a clamp away from the body.
  • Insufficient power steering fluid level.
  • Fill the power steering pump reservoir.
  • Improperly installed pump mounting.
  • Tighten the power steering pump attachment bolts.
Swishing Noise in the Steering Pump
  • Damaged power steering pump flow control valve.
  • Replace the power steering pump.
Whining Noise in the Steering Pump
  • Scores of the pressure plate and the vanes.
  • Replace the power steering pump.
  • Scores of the pump shaft bearing
  • Replace the power steering pump.

MAINTENANCE AND REPAIR

ON-VEHICLE SERVICE




Pump Drive Belt

Removal Procedure

  1. Remove the pump drive belt.
    • Match-mark on the pump and the rear shackle, in order to keep the tension equally before removal (1).
    • Loosen the rear shakle bolt (2).
    • Loosen the pump bolt (3).



    • Loosen the front shackle bolt (4).
    • Push the pump forward (5).



Installation Procedure

Install the pump drive belt with the bolts.

Tighten
  • Tighten the rear shackle bolt to 22 N•m(16 lb-ft) (a).
  • Tighten the power steering pump bolt to 22 N•m(16 lb-ft) (b).




Tighten
  • Tighten the front shackle bolt to 20 N•m(15 lb-ft) (c).




Pump Assembly

Removal Procedure

  1. Disconnect the pipe line from the pump.
    • Disconnect the suction hose (1).
    • Disconnect the pressure line pipe fitting (2).
  2. Remove the pump drive belt. Refer to "Pump Drive Belt" in this section.



  3. Remove the power steering pump.
    • Disconnect the power steering pressure switch connector (1).
    • Remove the rear shackle bolt (2).
    • Remove the pump bolt (3).



  4. Remove the front shackle bolt.



Installation Procedure

  1. Install the power steering pump with the bolt.
    • Install the bolts loosely to adjust the belt tension.
    • Install the pump bolt (1).
    • Install the rear shackle bolt (2).
    • Connect the power steering pressure switch connector (3).
  2. Install the pump drive belt. Refer to "Pump Drive Belt" in this section.



  3. Tighten the bolts.

    4. Tighten
      • Tighten the rear shackle bolt to 22 N•m (16 lb-ft) (a).
      • Tighten the pump bolt to 22 N•m (16 lb-ft) (b).




    • Tighten the front shakle bolt to 20 N•m (15 lb-ft) (c).
  4. Connect the pipe line to the pump.
    • Connect the pressure line pipe to the outlet connection on the steering pump.

    Tighten
    Tighten the pressure line pipe fitting to 28 N•m (21 lb-ft) (a).




      • Connect the suction hose (1).

    Notice : When adding fluid or making a complete fluid change, always use DEXRON®-II or DEXRON®-III power steering fluid. Failure to use the proper fluid will cause hose and seal damage and fluid leaks.

  5. Fill the fluid reservoir with power steering fluid.
  6. Inspect for leaks. If there are leaks, correct the cause of the leaks and bleed the system. Refer to Section 6A Bleeding the Power Steering System.

GENERAL DESCRIPTION AND SYSTEM OPERATION

Power Steering Pump

The power steering pump is a multivaned hydraulic pump.
The serpentine accessory drive belt on the engine drives the power steering pump.
The power steering pump provides the hydraulic pressure to the power steering gear.
The power steering gear uses the hydraulic pressure to assist in steering the vehicle.
The power steering system uses a remote reservoir in order to make available more space in the engine compartment.
